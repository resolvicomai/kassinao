# Product Hunt launch requirements and current guidance (2026)

Verified on 2026-07-14. This note prioritizes Product Hunt's own Launch Guide and Help Center. Product Hunt changes its submission flow frequently, so the live form remains authoritative where the official pages conflict.

## 1. Account eligibility

### Official facts

- The launch must be submitted from a **personal account**, not a company or branded account. Company accounts cannot submit, comment, or vote.
- The account must be **at least one week old**.
- A compliant profile uses a real first and last name, a clear personal photo, a personal bio/headline, and a unique non-company username.
- All submissions should be in **English**.
- A separate hunter is not required; Product Hunt recommends that makers submit their own product.

Sources: [Personal account vs company account](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/771527-personal-account-vs-company-account), [Community Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/3615694-community-guidelines), [Why was my comment or post removed?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/3539992-why-was-my-comment-or-post-removed), [How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product).

### Practical inference

- Before scheduling, make the maker profile look unmistakably human and complete. A product-logo avatar or branded username creates avoidable moderation risk.
- The account should have some genuine Product Hunt history before launch. Product Hunt officially recommends participating early, but does not publish a minimum number of comments or votes.

## 2. Submission fields

The current official checklist documents the following fields.

| Field | Official requirement or limit |
|---|---|
| Primary URL | Direct product/home/download URL. No shortened links and no tracking/UTM links. Avoid press or blog links as the primary URL. |
| Product name | Product name only. No description or emoji unless the emoji is legitimately part of the product name. |
| Tagline | Maximum **60 characters**. Plain, specific explanation; no gimmicks or exaggerated marketing language. |
| Description | The current Launch Guide says **500 characters**. A May 2025 Help Center article still says **260 characters**. See the conflict note below. |
| Launch tags/categories | Up to **3**, chosen for the strongest product fit. Product Hunt's pages alternately call these launch tags, topics, or categories. |
| Secondary links | Optional App Store, Google Play, Amazon, download, or similar links. |
| Product X handle | Optional; use the product account rather than the maker's personal account. |
| Thumbnail | Required. See asset specifications below. |
| Gallery | At least **2 images** for the gallery to be visible. The current Launch Guide calls two images required. |
| Video | Optional YouTube video. See asset specifications below. |
| Interactive demo | Optional Product Hunt-compatible demo link. |
| Makers | Identify yourself and add co-makers by their Product Hunt usernames. |
| Shoutouts | Up to **3** tools that materially helped build the product. |
| Pricing | Choose one of: **Free**, **Paid**, or **Paid (with a free trial or plan)**. |
| Product status | The flow can indicate beta/not fully released. However, Product Hunt currently prioritizes live products for homepage featuring. |
| Promo | Optional. If used, `What is the offer?`, `promo code`, and `expiration date` are all required. |
| First comment | Strongly recommended and can be prepared in the submission flow. |
| Draft/schedule | Current options are **Create Draft** or **Schedule Launch**. |

Primary source: [Preparing for launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch). Supporting sources: [How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product), [How to add a category](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/8104478-how-to-add-a-category-to-a-product), [How to add a promo code](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/4916443-how-to-add-a-promo-code).

### Description limit conflict

- **Official fact:** Product Hunt's live Launch Guide currently says 500 characters.
- **Official conflict:** its May 2025 Help Center article says 260 characters.
- **Safe inference:** prepare a strong version at or below 260 characters, then use the live form's displayed counter if more space is genuinely useful. Do not assume 500 until the form confirms it.

## 3. Logo, gallery, GIF, video, and demo specifications

### Thumbnail/logo

Official facts:

- Required and square; **240 × 240 px** is recommended.
- The live submission form currently enforces a **2 MB maximum**. Product Hunt's older written guidance still says under 3 MB, so the live form is the safer authority.
- An animated GIF only animates on hover, not automatically. Its first frame must therefore work as the static thumbnail.
- Avoid flashing/strobing, rapid cuts, and unreadable text. Product Hunt may edit unsuitable GIFs.
- Product Hunt reports that fewer than one third of Product of the Day launches used an animated thumbnail, so animation is not a prerequisite for success.

### Gallery

Official facts:

- At least **2 gallery images** are required for the gallery to be shown.
- Recommended canvas: **1270 × 760 px**.
- Animated GIFs are allowed.
- Multiple images can be uploaded together and reordered by drag and drop.

Not publicly specified in the official documentation reviewed:

- Maximum number of gallery assets.
- Gallery file-size limit.
- Exact accepted static file extensions.
- Exact aspect-ratio enforcement or cropping behavior.

The live uploader must be used as the authority for those points; do not infer unsupported limits.

### Video

Official facts:

- The launch gallery accepts a **full YouTube URL**; shortened YouTube links do not load.
- The video cannot be private and its settings must allow public sharing/embedding.
- A newly uploaded YouTube video may need roughly **12 hours** of processing before Product Hunt can integrate it.
- Product Hunt says about 53% of products that reached Product of the Day since 2021 included a video. This is correlation, not a guarantee.

### Interactive demo

Product Hunt currently documents launch-compatible demos from Arcade, Storylane, Hexus, Supademo, Layerpath, and ScreenSpace. Product Hunt says their unique Product Hunt demo links can be placed in the gallery.

Sources: [Preparing for launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch), [How to post a product](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/479557-how-to-post-a-product), [YouTube link troubleshooting](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/11869741-youtube-link-troubleshooting).

### Practical inference for a strong gallery

- Prefer a crisp static brand mark or a very subtle hover GIF for the thumbnail. A flashy GIF adds policy and readability risk without an official performance advantage.
- Use the first gallery image as a product/value-proposition cover, then show the real workflow, output, integrations, and trust/privacy evidence. Each frame should communicate one idea and remain understandable without narration.
- A short real-product demo is more persuasive than a cinematic brand video for a technical tool. This is a product-marketing inference, not a Product Hunt rule.

## 4. Maker comment

### Official facts

- The first maker comment appears directly below the gallery and can be prepared before launch.
- Product Hunt says **70%** of products that achieved Product of the Day, Week, or Month included a maker first comment.
- Product Hunt recommends covering the story, key features, ideal users/use cases, goals, pricing or offer, and the kind of feedback wanted.
- Ask for **feedback, not upvotes**.
- Images, videos, GIFs, and restrained emoji are allowed.
- Product Hunt recommends a humble, helpful, plainspoken tone rather than marketing language.

Source: [Preparing for launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch).

### Practical inference

A high-quality first comment should read like the maker personally explaining:

1. the real problem that triggered the product;
2. what the product does in one concrete workflow;
3. what makes it meaningfully different;
4. who should try it;
5. one or two specific questions for community feedback.

It should not read like a press release or contain a request to vote.

## 5. Pricing and promotions

### Official facts

- The pricing selector has exactly three broad choices: Free; Paid; Paid with a free trial or free plan.
- A Product Hunt promo is optional. If included, offer, code, and expiration date are mandatory.
- Product Hunt permits launch discounts, but a discount or giveaway must **never be conditional on voting**.

Sources: [Preparing for launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch), [How to add a promo code](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/4916443-how-to-add-a-promo-code), [Sharing your launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/sharing-your-launch).

### Practical inference

- Select the pricing label that matches the product users can actually access on launch day. Do not choose Free merely to improve click-through.
- A simple time-limited product discount may help conversion, but an improvised promotion can distract from the launch and create support obligations. It is not required for featuring.

## 6. Drafting, scheduling, and publishing behavior

### Official facts

- Product Hunt replaced `Launch Now` with **Create Draft**; the current submission completion options are Create Draft and Schedule Launch.
- A launch can be scheduled for a day within the next **30 days**.
- Scheduled launches remain editable under `My Products`.
- A scheduled launch is not indexed and cannot receive upvotes before it goes live.
- By default, a scheduled launch is visible only to associated makers and product-team members. The lock control can change it to `Public`, allowing anyone with the link to preview it.
- Product Hunt operates on a 24-hour Pacific-time cycle. Its guide recommends the first minute of the Product Hunt day, described as **12:01 AM PST**, for a full launch-day window.
- Product Hunt may edit a post's name/tagline, thumbnail, description, or gallery to improve clarity or formatting.
- A submitted product does not have a self-service delete button; removal generally requires Product Hunt support.

Sources: [How to schedule a post](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/2724119-how-to-schedule-a-post), [Where did Launch Now go?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/9823193-where-did-launch-now-go), [How to share a scheduled launch](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/15706445-how-to-share-a-scheduled-launch), [Preparing for launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch), [Why did my post content change?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/8858588-why-did-my-post-content-change), [Can you remove my post?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484941-can-you-remove-my-post).

### Timing caveat and inference

- Product Hunt's own documentation inconsistently uses `PST` even during periods when U.S. Pacific time normally observes daylight saving. The date/time displayed in the live scheduling UI is therefore the final authority for conversion to Brazil time.
- Schedule only after the complete preview has been reviewed. Creating or editing a draft is reversible; scheduling commits a public date and eventual publication.

## 7. Homepage featuring criteria

### Official facts current as of March 2026

Product Hunt says it primarily features live digital products and evaluates whether a launch is:

- useful;
- novel;
- high craft;
- creative.

It does not normally feature waitlisted products without immediate access, services, directories/lists, templates, boilerplates, courses, reports, events, commerce stores, deal sites, or off-topic products. Products offering only an email signup are not eligible for the homepage. Engagement alone does not determine featuring.

Sources: [Product Hunt Featuring Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/9883485-product-hunt-featuring-guidelines), [Can I submit an unreleased product?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484932-can-i-submit-an-unreleased-product).

### Practical inference

- The landing page should let reviewers understand and try the live product immediately. A login/access wall with no useful walkthrough or demo may weaken the submission even if it is technically live.
- The submission should foreground the product itself, not present it as a consulting service or a traffic-only landing page.

## 8. Voting and promotion policy

### Official facts: prohibited

- Asking people directly to upvote.
- Mass messaging by email, DM, or social platforms asking for votes.
- Incentives, discounts, freebies, contests, or giveaways tied to an upvote.
- Coordinated voting campaigns, voting rings, fake accounts, bots, or other artificial activity.
- Scraped unsolicited outreach or spam.
- Self-promotion in unrelated Product Hunt comments.

Product Hunt monitors unusual patterns automatically, accepts community reports, and performs manual review. It can remove invalid votes, reduce ranking, unfeature or remove a launch, restrict accounts, or suspend access. One upvote does not necessarily equal one ranking point; authenticity and broader community engagement affect points.

### Official facts: allowed and encouraged

- Share the direct launch link organically on social media, newsletters, in-app notices, and communities where the maker is already active.
- Invite people to try the product, join the discussion, leave meaningful feedback, comment, share their experience, or help spread the word.
- Respond quickly and genuinely to Product Hunt comments.
- Run a promotion that is independent of voting.

Sources: [Community Guidelines](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/3615694-community-guidelines), [How do I share my post?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/2690626-how-do-i-share-my-post), [Can I ask my community to upvote?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484935-can-i-ask-my-community-friends-family-to-upvote-a-product), [Voting integrity](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/11869098-how-does-product-hunt-ensure-fair-voting-and-prevent-spam-or-vote-manipulation), [Sharing your launch](https://www.producthunt.com/launch/sharing-your-launch), [How is the homepage ranked?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484938-how-is-the-homepage-ranked).

### Safe wording inference

Use calls to action such as `Try it and tell us what breaks`, `Join the conversation`, or `We would value your feedback`. Do not use `Upvote us`, `Vote for us`, `Help us reach #1`, or disguised equivalents.

## 9. Relaunch and duplicate restrictions

### Official facts

- Product Hunt currently asks makers to wait at least **six months** between launches from the same product or company and requires a significant product update.
- Products sharing the same root domain are subject to the same six-month gap.
- A new UI or pricing change alone is not considered significant.

Source: [Can I relaunch my product?](https://help.producthunt.com/en/articles/484934-can-i-relaunch-my-product).

## 10. Final authoritative checklist before scheduling

- Personal account is at least one week old and profile is complete.
- Entire submission is in English.
- Product is live and accessible enough to evaluate immediately.
- Primary URL is clean: no shortener and no UTM.
- Product name is name-only.
- Tagline is 60 characters or fewer.
- Description fits the live form counter; a 260-character version is ready.
- No more than three strong launch tags/categories.
- Square 240 × 240 thumbnail is under the live form's 2 MB limit and its first frame works statically.
- At least two 1270 × 760 gallery assets are uploaded and ordered.
- YouTube demo, if used, is fully processed, embeddable, non-private, and linked with its full URL.
- Pricing label is truthful; any promo includes offer, code, and expiration.
- Maker and any co-makers are correctly credited.
- First comment is personal, useful, and asks for feedback rather than votes.
- Scheduled preview is reviewed at desktop and mobile widths before selecting a launch date.
- Promotion copy contains no vote request, incentive, coordination, or spam.
